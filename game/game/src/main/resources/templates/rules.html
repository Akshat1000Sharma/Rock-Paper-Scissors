Rules of the Game

This is a simple game of rock, paper, scissors.

The rules of rock, paper, scissors are:

Hand signals:
Players use three hand signals to represent rock, paper, and scissors:
- Rock: A closed fist
- Paper: A flat hand with fingers and thumb extended, and the palm facing down
- Scissors: A fist with the index and middle fingers fully extended toward the opponent

Winning:
Rock beats scissors, paper beats rock, and scissors beats paper. If both players show the same hand signal, it's a tie.

Playing:
Players stand opposite each other and count "rock, paper, scissors" together. On the word "scissors", both players put out their hand signal.

Zero-sum game:
Rock, paper, scissors is a zero-sum game, meaning one player wins and the other loses.
Some say that a good strategy is to not choose the same thing twice in a row, especially if you've been beaten.
